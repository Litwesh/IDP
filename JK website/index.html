About Jammu & Kashmir

Jammu & Kashmir

Jammu and Kashmir comprises of the southern portion of the broader Kashmir area, which has been the subject of a dispute between India and Pakistan since 1947 and between India and China since 1959. It is a region managed by India as a union territory. In the west and north, the Pakistani-administrated regions of Gilgit-Baltistan and Azad Kashmir are divided from Jammu and Kashmir by the Line of Control. It is located to the north of the Indian states of Punjab and Himachal Pradesh and to the west of the Indian union territory of Ladakh.

The two areas it includes, the Kashmir Valley and the Jammu region, are known collectively as Jammu and Kashmir. Jammu and Kashmir is referred to as a portion of "Indian illegally occupied Jammu and Kashmir" (IIOJK), "Indian-occupied Kashmir" (IOK), or "Indian-held Kashmir" (IHK) by the Government of Pakistan and sources in Pakistan. The territory governed by Pakistan is referred to as "Pakistan-occupied Kashmir" (POK) or "Pakistan-held Kashmir" (PHK) by the Indian government and Indian sources. Neutral sources frequently use the terms "Indian-controlled Kashmir" and "Indian-administered Kashmir."
